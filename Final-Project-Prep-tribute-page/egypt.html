Rabbi Ovadia Yosef
Early Life Residing in Egypt Return to Israel Rishon Lezion Approach

In 1947, he was invited to Cairo by Rabbi Aharon Choueka, founder of yeshiva 'Ahavah VeAchvah', to teach in his yeshiva. He also served, at the request of Rabbi Ben-Zion Meir Hai Uziel, as head of the Cairo beth din (rabbinical court).

He found that religious observance was lax in Egypt, be it the Jewish community at large, or even its rabbis. One of the major Halachic issues was the lack of any organised system of Kashrut, which led to conflict between him and other members of the community. It was due to these events that he resigned from his position, just two years after arriving in Cairo.

Approximately one year later he returned to Israel.
